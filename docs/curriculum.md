Spot the Red Flags — Curriculum (Modules 0–14 + Practice Labs)

Principles
- Plain English: short sentences, define jargon inline, actionable checks.
- Every metric ties to an actionable question or document request.
- Focus audience: early-career legal professionals.
- Each module includes: Learning goal, Plain-English summary, Key checks, Actionable requests, Short quiz idea.

Module 0 — Introduction & Foundations
- Goal: Explain forensic accounting in plain English and why it matters legally.
- Summary: Forensic accounting looks for manipulation, follows money, and assesses intent.
- Key checks: Does reported profit match cash collected? Ask: "Show cash receipts and bank statements for the period."
- Actionable request: Request trial balance, bank statements, and revenue contracts.
- Quiz idea: Define "materiality" in one sentence and name two GAAP principles.

Module 1 — Financial Statement Literacy & GAAP Basics
- Goal: Teach Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow, and core GAAP rules.
- Summary: Balance Sheet lists resources and claims. Income shows performance. Cash Flow shows actual cash.
- Key checks: Matching (revenues with expenses), Conservatism (be cautious), Materiality (what matters).
- Actionable request: Ask for reconciliations between sub-ledgers and financial statements.
- Quiz idea: Given a small sample, mark which items affect cash versus profit.

Module 2 — Revenue & Receivables Risks
- Goal: Spot risky revenue recognition and receivable problems.
- Summary: Look for invoiced but undelivered sales, side letters, or rising DSO.
- Key checks: Bill-and-hold evidence, unusual shipment terms, DSO trending up.
- Actionable request: Ask for contracts, shipping docs, and customer acceptance records.
- Quiz idea: Identify which documents prove delivery vs. billing.

Module 3 — Expenses & Reserve Manipulation
- Goal: Find under-accruals, one-time charges, or smoothing of expenses.
- Summary: Watch for low reserves, sudden large charges, and timing tricks.
- Key checks: Sudden reserve drops, unusual "one-time" items, variance analysis vs. budget.
- Actionable request: Request expense backup, accrual schedules, and board minutes describing reserves.
- Quiz idea: Choose whether a charge is likely recurring or a one-time adjustment, and why.

Module 4 — Assets & Capitalization
- Goal: Detect improper capitalization and delayed impairments.
- Summary: Some costs should be expensed, not capitalized; check policy and supporting docs.
- Key checks: Large capital additions without supporting invoices, long-lived asset impairment delays.
- Actionable request: Ask for project cost detail and capitalization policy.
- Quiz idea: Given a project spend, decide capitalize vs. expense with short rationale.

Module 5 — Liabilities & Off–Balance-Sheet Risks
- Goal: Uncover undisclosed obligations (guarantees, SPEs/VIEs, contingencies).
- Summary: Some liabilities hide off the balance sheet; look for guarantees and related parties.
- Key checks: Footnote language, related-party disclosures, guarantees in contracts.
- Actionable request: Ask for loan agreements, contracts with variable payments, and related-party schedules.
- Quiz idea: Spot language in a footnote that indicates a hidden obligation.

Module 6 — Cash Flow Quality
- Goal: Compare net income vs. operating cash flow to test earnings quality.
- Summary: If profit rises but operating cash is flat or falling, investigate.
- Key checks: Cash-to-earnings ratio, large add-backs in cash flow from operations.
- Actionable request: Request bank reconciliations and cash receipts detail.
- Quiz idea: Calculate cash-to-income for a short sample and interpret it in one sentence.

Module 7 — Related-Party & Circular Transactions
- Goal: Spot non-arm’s-length deals and circular trades.
- Summary: Related parties can shift results; look for unusual terms and round-tripping.
- Key checks: Party relationships in registries, repeated transactions with the same counterparties.
- Actionable request: Ask for counterparty IDs, contracts, and board approvals.
- Quiz idea: Given a set of transactions, identify which look circular.

Module 8 — Ratio & Trend Analysis Techniques
- Goal: Teach DSO, inventory turnover, debt-to-equity, common-size, and trend basics.
- Summary: Ratios reveal changes over time and versus peers.
- Key checks: Compute DSO and compare to industry benchmarks; spot sudden jumps.
- Actionable request: Ask for AR aging and sales detail by customer.
- Quiz idea: Compute a simple ratio and explain the next evidence request.

Module 9 — Investigative Techniques & Sampling
- Goal: Introduce Benford’s Law, source testing, and sampling templates.
- Summary: Use statistical checks, then confirm with source documents.
- Key checks: Unusual digit distributions, repeated amounts, missing supporting docs.
- Actionable request: Request raw transaction exports and supporting PDFs.
- Quiz idea: Short Benford example and decide if follow-up is needed.

Module 10 — Auditor Signals & Non-GAAP Issues
- Goal: Read audit reports, going-concern warnings, and restatement signs.
- Summary: Auditors’ language often signals deeper problems.
- Key checks: Qualified opinions, management letter points, restatements trend.
- Actionable request: Ask for auditor communications and management responses.
- Quiz idea: Interpret one sentence from an auditor's report.

Module 11 — Legal Context: Diligence, Filings, Escalation
- Goal: Tie indicators to legal actions: M&A diligence, securities filings, escalation steps.
- Summary: Translate accounting indicators into legal questions and escalation steps.
- Key checks: Material misstatement risk, disclosure adequacy, internal control weaknesses.
- Actionable request: Prepare an evidence package and notify the audit committee.
- Quiz idea: Draft a short escalation note given a finding.

Module 12 — Practice Labs & Simulations
- Content: Interactive labs (Enron SPE case, channel-stuffing simulation). Branching scenarios with feedback.
- Learning: Make decisions (request docs, interview people) and see consequences.
- Assessment: Score by evidence quality and recommended next steps.

Module 13 — AI-Assisted Financial Review Tool (Design)
- Content: Use the tool to load statements, run checks (DSO, cash vs. net income), parse footnotes for trigger terms.
- Learning: Interpret AI-suggested indicators in plain English and ask for supporting documents.
- Safety: Tool outputs are "indicators for further investigation only"—always include the disclaimer.

Module 14 — Capstone Assessment
- Activity: Full review using AI tool, prepare short issues memo with evidence and requests.
- Grading: Rubric for evidence cited, plain-English interpretation, and legal next steps.
